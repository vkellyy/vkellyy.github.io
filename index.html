time capsule

Gen X (65-79)
Millennial (80-94)
Gen Z (95-15)

gen x

buggin

Verb


Freaking out, or losing your cool.


Quit buggin out I got this under control.

bones

Noun


Used to refer to currency or cigarettes.


How many bones do I owe you?

crib

Noun


A place of residence.


Link me at my crib tonight.

old school

Adjective


Old fashioned or retro.


Those shoes are completely old school.

going postal

Verb


Coined after a post office worker had a mental breakdown and attacked his coworkers. Usually means going crazy.


I had class for 8 hours straight today. I think I’m gonna go postal.

dip

Verb


To leave, or go somewhere else.


I gotta dip.

iight

Preposition


Another way to say alright.


How was the movie? It was iight.

eat my shorts

Interjection


Up yours. A verbal way to give someone the finger.


You better spot me for lunch today. Eat my shorts!

f bomb

Interjection


Used in place of an expletive, specifically "fuck".


Yo I wanna drop the f-bomb so bad right now.

fresh

Adjective


New or nice.


That new Bronze hoodie is fresh.

millennial

woke

Adjective


A word used to describe a person's awareness to injustice in society.


My Professor gave a super woke lecture today about the current events in American politics.

ghost

Verb


To avoid or end an interaction with someone by cutting off all connections to them.


Emily was so mad that Josh ghosted her out of nowhere, right when she thought things were going great.

gassed up

Verb


Refers to being hyped up or excited.


Man, I’m all gassed up for this concert on friday.

on fleek

Adjective


To be very good or attractive.


Damn! Her eyebrows are on fleek today.

finesse

Verb


To get something taken or stolen.


I can’t find my wallet anywhere, I must’ve been finessed.

yeet

Verb


To get rid of something quickly.


Kayla just decided to yeet her whole binder in the trash after she took her test.

finna

Verb


Going or intending to.


I’m finna go to bed after this class.

ship it

Verb


To imagine and support two people being in a relationship.


Dan and Jenny would be really cute together, I definitely ship it.

lit

Adjective


When something fun or exciting is happening.


Yo so I was at this party on Saturday and it was super lit.

gen z

salty

Adjective


Jealous or particularly upset.


He’s just salty he didn’t get the color he wanted.

shook

Adjective


Scared; Often used to describe people who aren’t as tough as they say they are.


Nah man he’s not about it he’s shook.

bussin

Adjective


Really good, or a lot.


Yo that chopped cheese had my head bussin.

thirsty

Adjective


Craving something.


Bro that kid is so thirsty he’s been all over her all night.

jakes

Noun


Another word for cops.


Yo big jake on that corner.

clipped

Adverb


It’s over or not worth it.


Bro its clipped for class today I didn’t do any of my work.

deadass

Adverb


Used to pose seriousness or tone, and can directly mean “Are you serious?”. Usually posed as a question or a statement.


I deadass have had it with this jawn.

jawn

Noun


Overall it refers to a thing, but also often translates to a girl.


Did you finish your sandwich? Nah that jawn was foul.

jit

Noun


Refers to someone of a young age, but can also be an insult-calling someone immature or small.


He’s been skating since he was a jit.

big smoke

Interjection


Something you say when something goes wrong.


I tried to heel flip over that gap and completely ate it. Big smoke!

bop

Adverb


Not worth it.


Are you trying to go out tomorrow? Nah man that’s such a bop.

deep

Adverb


Constitutes a long distance.


Yo that’s so deep from here!
